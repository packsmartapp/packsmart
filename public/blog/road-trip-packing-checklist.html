There's nothing quite like the freedom of a road trip — open windows, good music, and the promise of adventure around every bend. But that freedom quickly turns into frustration when you're 200 kilometres from the nearest town and realise you forgot your phone charger, snacks, or worse, your driver's licence.

Whether you're planning a weekend getaway, a cross-country adventure, or a scenic coastal drive, this is the only road trip packing checklist you'll ever need. We've organised everything by category so you can pack smarter, not heavier.

1. Car Essentials & Safety Gear

Your car is your lifeline on a road trip. Before you think about clothes or snacks, make sure your vehicle is ready for the journey. A quick pre-trip check can prevent breakdowns and keep everyone safe.

Pre-Trip Vehicle Check

About a week before departure, run through these basics. If anything seems off, get it checked by a mechanic.

  • Check engine oil level and top up if needed
  • Inspect tyre pressure (including the spare)
  • Test all lights — headlights, brake lights, indicators
  • Top up windshield washer fluid
  • Check brake fluid and coolant levels
  • Inspect windshield wipers for wear
  • Ensure air conditioning works (you'll thank yourself later)

Safety & Emergency Kit

  • First aid kit (bandages, antiseptic, pain relievers, any personal medications)
  • Jumper cables or portable jump starter
  • Spare tyre + jack + lug wrench
  • Tyre pressure gauge
  • Reflective warning triangles or road flares
  • Torch (flashlight) with extra batteries
  • Duct tape (the universal fixer)
  • Basic tool kit (screwdrivers, pliers, adjustable wrench)
  • Tow rope or strap
  • Fire extinguisher (small, car-rated)
Pro Tip: Keep your emergency kit in a dedicated bag in the boot (trunk). A pre-packed roadside emergency kit from an auto store covers most of these items in one compact bag.

2. Documents & Money

These are the things that can end your trip before it starts if you forget them. Do a quick wallet and documents check the night before you leave.

Short trip? See our weekend trip packing list for lighter packing.

  • Valid driver's licence
  • Vehicle registration papers
  • Insurance documents / roadside assistance card
  • Passport or ID card (especially for cross-border trips)
  • Cash in small denominations (for tolls, parking, emergencies)
  • Credit / debit cards
  • Copies of hotel reservations or campsite bookings
  • Emergency contact list (on paper, not just your phone)
Important: Many toll roads and remote petrol stations still only accept cash. Keep some small notes handy rather than relying entirely on cards or digital wallets.

3. Clothing & Footwear

The beauty of road trips is that you can pack more than you would for a flight. But resist the urge to bring your entire wardrobe. Stick to versatile, comfortable pieces that mix and match easily.

Clothing Essentials

  • Comfortable driving clothes (soft trousers, breathable tops)
  • Layers — a light jacket, hoodie, or fleece
  • Undergarments (one per day plus a spare)
  • Socks (merino wool or moisture-wicking for comfort)
  • Sleepwear
  • Swimsuit (you never know when you'll find a lake or pool)
  • Rain jacket or poncho
  • One "nice" outfit (for dinners or unexpected plans)
  • Hat or cap for sun protection
  • Sunglasses

Footwear

  • Comfortable driving shoes (slip-ons are great)
  • Walking shoes or trainers
  • Sandals or flip-flops (for rest stops, beaches, showers)
  • Hiking boots (if trails are on the itinerary)
Packing Hack: Use packing cubes to keep your bag organised. Keep a separate bag for dirty laundry — your clean clothes will stay fresh, and you won't have to sort through everything when it's time to do a wash.

4. Toiletries & Personal Care

Unlike flying, there are no liquid restrictions on a road trip. Bring your full-size products if you want — your car's boot has plenty of room.

Road tripping with family? Our family travel tips has tips.

  • Toothbrush + toothpaste
  • Shampoo + conditioner
  • Body wash or soap
  • Deodorant
  • Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
  • Insect repellent
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • Wet wipes / hand sanitiser
  • Tissues / toilet paper (trust us on this one)
  • Moisturiser
  • Any prescription medications
  • Contact lenses / glasses (plus spares)
  • Hairbrush or comb
  • Nail clippers
Don't Forget: Keep a small "quick access" toiletry pouch in the cabin — hand sanitiser, wet wipes, tissues, and lip balm. You'll reach for these constantly during driving stretches.

5. Tech & Navigation

A dead phone in the middle of nowhere is a road tripper's worst nightmare. Plan your tech setup so you stay charged, connected, and on-route the whole way.

  • Phone car charger (multi-port if travelling with others)
  • Portable power bank (fully charged before departure)
  • Phone mount / dashboard holder
  • Charging cables for all devices
  • Offline maps downloaded (Google Maps or equivalent)
  • Bluetooth FM transmitter (if your car lacks Bluetooth)
  • Camera (or just your phone)
  • Portable Wi-Fi hotspot (for remote areas)
  • Car USB adapter / cigarette lighter adapter
Essential App: Download your route's maps for offline use before you leave. Many scenic routes pass through areas with zero mobile signal. Also grab a petrol price comparison app to save money along the way.

6. Comfort & Entertainment

The difference between a good road trip and a great one is comfort. These small items make long drives feel a lot shorter.

Pack smarter with our minimalist packing guide.

  • Neck pillow / travel pillow
  • Blanket or throw
  • Reusable bags (for shopping, laundry, rubbish)
  • Earplugs and eye mask (for passenger naps)
  • Playlist, podcasts, or audiobooks downloaded
  • Car games / travel games (for groups)
  • Journal or notebook
  • Book or e-reader
  • Trash bag (keep the car clean — use a small bin or bag hooked to the seat)
  • Car air freshener
  • Umbrella
Comfort Hack: Take a 15-minute break every two hours of driving. Step out, stretch your legs, and move your body. Your back, neck, and focus will all thank you. Short breaks actually help you arrive faster by keeping you alert.

7. Snacks & Drinks

Good snacks are the fuel that keeps a road trip fun. Pack a mix of quick energy bites and filling options so you're not stopping at every petrol station.

  • Reusable water bottles (at least 1 litre per person)
  • Cooler bag or small cooler with ice packs
  • Trail mix / nuts
  • Fresh fruit (apples, bananas, grapes)
  • Granola bars or energy bars
  • Sandwiches or wraps (pre-made for day one)
  • Crackers and cheese
  • Chocolate or sweets (for morale)
  • Coffee or tea thermos
  • Paper towels / napkins
  • Reusable cutlery set
Hydration Reminder: Dehydration causes fatigue, headaches, and poor concentration — all dangerous when driving. Aim to drink water every hour, even if you don't feel thirsty.

8. Road Tripping with Kids

Road trips with children require a bit more preparation, but the memories are absolutely worth it. The key is keeping them comfortable, entertained, and well-fed.

Crossing borders? Review our international travel checklist.

  • Car seat or booster seat (age-appropriate)
  • Tablet or device with downloaded shows and games
  • Headphones (kid-safe volume-limited ones)
  • Colouring books, crayons, stickers
  • Favourite stuffed animal or comfort toy
  • Kid-friendly snacks (crackers, fruit pouches, juice boxes)
  • Spare change of clothes (easily accessible)
  • Plastic bags (for motion sickness emergencies)
  • Baby wipes (useful even for older kids)
  • Sunshade for back windows
  • Travel potty (for toddlers on remote routes)
Parent Tip: Plan stops at interesting points rather than just petrol stations. A playground, a scenic viewpoint, or even a quirky roadside attraction gives kids something to look forward to and burns off energy.

9. Winter Road Trip Add-Ons

Cold weather road trips need extra preparation. Conditions can change rapidly, and being prepared for the worst means you can enjoy the best.

  • Ice scraper + snow brush
  • De-icer spray for windshield and locks
  • Tyre chains (required in many mountainous regions)
  • Warm blankets (one per person)
  • Thermal base layers
  • Insulated gloves and warm hat
  • Hand and toe warmers
  • Small snow shovel
  • Bag of sand or cat litter (for traction on ice)
  • Extra windshield washer fluid (winter formula)
  • Thermos with hot drinks
Safety First: Check road conditions and weather forecasts before driving through mountain passes or remote stretches in winter. Let someone know your planned route and expected arrival time.

10. 7 Common Road Trip Packing Mistakes

Even experienced road trippers fall into these traps. Avoid them and your trip will be smoother from start to finish.

1. Overpacking clothes

You're not walking a runway. Pack clothes that layer well and can be worn more than once. Laundromats exist along most routes if you're gone more than a week.

2. Forgetting offline maps

Scenic routes and mountain passes often have zero mobile signal. Download your entire route's maps to your phone before departure. It takes two minutes and can save your trip.

3. No emergency cash

Card machines fail. Remote areas might be cash-only. Always keep some small notes tucked away separately from your wallet.

4. Skipping the pre-trip car check

A flat tyre or overheated engine 300 km from a mechanic is a nightmare. Spend 30 minutes checking your car before leaving — it's the best investment in your trip.

5. Packing everything in the boot

Keep a "drive bag" within arm's reach: snacks, water, phone charger, sunglasses, wet wipes, and tissues. Stopping and opening the boot every time you need something gets old fast.

6. Ignoring the weather forecast

Weather can change dramatically along a long route. Check forecasts for every major stop, not just your destination. Pack layers and rain gear regardless of the season.

7. No backup charging

A portable power bank is essential. If your car charger fails or you're out exploring, a dead phone means no navigation, no camera, and no emergency calls.

Here's a condensed version you can screenshot or save. For a fully personalised version based on your specific route, weather, and travel style, try PackSmart.

Car & Safety

First aid kit, jumper cables, spare tyre + jack, torch, duct tape, reflective triangles, tool kit, tow rope, tyre pressure gauge, fire extinguisher.

Documents

Clothing

Comfort & Food

Neck pillow, blanket, water bottles, cooler, snacks (nuts, fruit, bars, sandwiches), coffee thermos, trash bag, reusable bags, umbrella.

Start Packing, Start Driving

A well-packed car is the foundation of a stress-free road trip. You don't need to bring everything on this list — just the items that match your route, season, and travel style. The goal is to be prepared without being buried in gear.

The best road trips aren't about perfection. They're about freedom, flexibility, and the confidence that comes from knowing you've got everything you need. So check this list, load up the car, queue up your favourite playlist, and hit the road.
