Japan Travel & Packing Guide 2026

Complete Checklist for Indian Travelers

🚢 Visa & Documents for Japan

Indian citizens can visit Japan visa-free for up to 90 days under the Temporary Visitor category. However, if you plan to stay longer or need a work visa, here's what you need:

Visa Application Process

  • Processing Time: 5-7 business days (standard), 2-3 days (expedited at extra cost)
  • Cost: ₹3,500-5,000 per person through Japan embassy
  • Required Documents: Passport (6 months validity), visa application form, passport photo, travel itinerary, proof of financial means, accommodation proof
  • Embassy Locations in India: New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Bangalore
💡 Pro Tip: Japanese embassies in India now accept online applications. Register at the visa portal to avoid long queues. Keep copies of all documents in separate folders.

✨ Japan Weather by Season

Japan experiences four distinct seasons. Choose your visit based on what you want to experience:

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Cherry Blossoms: Peak bloom early April

Temp: 10-20°C

Packing: Light layers, jacket

Best For: Photographers & nature lovers

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Humid & Hot: Festival season

Temp: 25-35°C

Packing: Light fabrics, sunscreen, umbrella

Best For: Culture festivals, fireworks

🍁 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Foliage: Stunning fall colors

Temp: 15-25°C

Packing: Layers, light sweater

Best For: Hiking & sightseeing

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold & Dry: Fewer crowds

Temp: 0-10°C

Packing: Warm coat, gloves, thermal wear

Best For: Budget travelers, skiing

🚧 Complete Packing List for Japan

Clothing & Footwear

  • Walking Shoes (Critical!): Japan involves extensive walking on hard pavements. Bring 2-3 pairs of comfortable, broken-in shoes with good cushioning. Temples require shoe removal, so pack easy-to-remove footwear.
  • Socks (many pairs – temples, hotels, hot springs require sock removal)
  • Temple-appropriate clothing: Long pants/skirts, shoulders covered
  • Lightweight layers for varying temperatures
  • Light rain jacket (Japan is humid)
  • One pair of formal clothes for nicer restaurants
  • Undergarments (7-10 days worth, laundry available everywhere)

Toiletries & Personal Care

  • Deodorant (hard to find in Japan)
  • Prescription medications (bring 2x supply)
  • Specific skincare products you're loyal to
  • Toothbrush & toothpaste (hotels provide)
  • Hair care products (if you have specific needs)
  • Feminine hygiene products (available but expensive)

Electronics

  • Power Adapter: Japan uses Type A & B plugs (110V). Bring universal adapter or purchase in Japan (¥500-1,000)
  • Pocket WiFi rental: ¥1,000-1,500/day from Narita/Kansai airport rental counters
  • eSIM cards: Docomo/SoftBank/au eSIMs cost ¥3,000-5,000 for 10GB
  • Phone charger & power bank
  • Camera (if not using smartphone)

Money & Banking

  • Japanese Yen (JPY): 1 INR ≈ 0.79 JPY (rates fluctuate)
  • Japan is still very cash-heavy – many small shops, temples, and restaurants don't accept cards
  • ATMs at 7-Eleven: Withdraw cash 24/7. International card fees: ¥110-210 per transaction
  • Carry ¥10,000-20,000 (₹8,000-16,000) minimum cash
  • IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo): Load ¥2,000-10,000 for trains, buses, shops
⚠️ Important: Do NOT rely only on credit/debit cards. Always carry cash. Many temples, small restaurants, and local shops in Kyoto/Osaka are cash-only.

❌ What NOT to Pack (Buy in Japan Instead)

  • Toiletries: Daiso (100 Yen shop) and Don Quijôte have excellent shampoo, conditioner, deodorant, skincare at great prices
  • Umbrella: Every konbini (convenience store) sells cheap umbrellas (¥500-1,000). No need to carry
  • Medications: Drugstores like Matsumoto Kiyoshi have OTC drugs, pain relief, cold medicine
  • Chargers: Available everywhere in 100 Yen shops
  • Comfortable shoes for walking: Actually, bring yours! Japanese sizes run small

📄 Customs Rules & Restrictions

  • Limit: Max ¥1,000,000 (or equivalent) in currency without declaration
  • No prohibited items: Weapons, drugs, counterfeit goods, obscene materials
  • Food restrictions: Fresh produce, meat, dairy products restricted (packaged snacks OK)
  • Electronics duty-free for personal use
  • Keep receipts for souvenirs (tax refund for foreign visitors)

🇮🇳 Quick Tips for Indian Travelers

Vegetarian Food Challenges

Vegetarian options are limited in Japan. Dashi (fish stock) is in most dishes:

  • Go to Indian restaurants (Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto have many)
  • Eat vegetable ramen (specify no meat/dashi)
  • Konbini has vegetable onigiri, edamame, tempura vegetables
  • Curry shops often have vegetarian curries

Train Etiquette

  • No phone calls on trains (considered rude)
  • Priority seats (blue seats) for elderly, pregnant, disabled
  • Let people exit before boarding
  • Keep bags close on crowded trains

Cultural Dos & Don'ts

  • Shoe Removal: Remove shoes at temples, homes, ryokans, hot springs
  • Tipping is Rude: Never tip. Service charge is included
  • Chopstick Rules: Never stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral ritual)
  • Convenience Stores are Amazing: Fresh food, 24-hour availability, cheap, delicious
  • Trash Shortage: Bring a small bag for trash. Public bins are rare
💡 Pro Tip: Learn basic Japanese phrases: "Arigatou gozaimasu" (thank you), "Sumimasen" (excuse me), "Eigo o hanasemasu ka?" (Do you speak English?). Respect goes a long way!

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Indians need a visa for Japan?
Yes, Indians need a tourist visa for Japan. Apply at the Japanese Embassy or Consulate in India with your passport, itinerary, hotel bookings, bank statements (showing sufficient funds), and a letter of employment. Processing takes 5-7 working days. Japan does not offer visa on arrival or e-visa for Indian passport holders.
What should I pack for Japan from India?
Pack layers — Japan has four distinct seasons. Bring comfortable walking shoes (you'll walk 15,000+ steps daily), a compact umbrella, a small towel (public restrooms may not have paper towels), a pocket Wi-Fi or SIM card, IC card for trains, and modest clothing for shrines. In winter (December-March), bring a warm coat, thermals, and gloves.
What is the best time to visit Japan from India?
Spring (March-May) for cherry blossoms and autumn (October-November) for fall foliage are the most popular times. Summer (June-August) is hot and humid with a rainy season in June-July. Winter (December-February) is great for skiing and hot springs. Cherry blossom season in late March to mid-April is the most iconic time to visit.
How much does a Japan trip cost from India?
A 7-day Japan trip costs approximately ₹80,000-1,20,000 per person on a budget, ₹1,20,000-2,00,000 mid-range, and ₹2,00,000+ luxury. Return flights from India cost ₹30,000-55,000. Japan is expensive but a 7-day JR Pass (¥50,000/₹28,000) saves hugely on bullet trains. Budget meals cost ₹400-700, and capsule hotels start at ₹2,000/night.
