The Maldives — turquoise water, overwater villas, coral reefs, and the kind of peace that makes you forget your phone exists. And, it's one of the easiest international trips: just 4 hours from most Indian cities, free visa on arrival for up to 90 days, and direct flights from multiple cities.

But here's what most guides don't tell you: the Maldives isn't just for luxury honeymooners. Budget guesthouses on local islands have made it accessible for everyone. This guide covers both resort luxury and budget island stays, with a packing list tailored.

📋 Visa & Documents for Indians

Great news: Indians get a free visa on arrival in the Maldives for up to 90 days. No application, no fee, no hassle. Just land at Velána International Airport (Malé) and walk through immigration.

DocumentDetails
PassportValid for at least 6 months from arrival
Return/onward ticketMandatory — they check at immigration
Hotel/resort bookingConfirmed accommodation for entire stay (resort, guesthouse, or liveaboard)
Sufficient funds$100 + $50/day (rarely checked but technically required)
Visa feeFREE for 30 days (extendable to 90 days)
💡 Pro Tip: The IMUGA Traveller Declaration form must be filled online within 96 hours before arrival. Do it at imuga.immigration.gov.mv. It takes 5 minutes and saves time at immigration.

👚 The Complete Maldives Packing List

Clothing

  • 3–4 light tops — Cotton or linen. You'll mostly be in resort wear or swimwear
  • 2–3 shorts — For resort/guesthouse areas
  • 3+ swimsuits — You'll be in the water multiple times a day. Having extras to dry is essential
  • 1 cover-up/sarong — For walking between pool and restaurant
  • 1 modest outfit — Long pants/skirt and covered shoulders for visiting Malé or local islands (the Maldives is a Muslim country)
  • 1 nice outfit — For resort dinners (most resorts have smart-casual dress codes at dinner)
  • Reef-safe water shoes — Coral is sharp. You'll thank yourself during beach entries and snorkeling
  • Flip-flops — Your main footwear. Some resorts are entirely barefoot
⚠️ Dress Code on Local Islands: The Maldives is an Islamic nation. On local (inhabited) islands, dress modestly — cover shoulders and knees. Bikinis are only allowed at designated "bikini beaches" on local islands and at resorts. On resort islands, wear whatever you want.

Snorkeling & Water Gear

  • Snorkel mask & tube — Resorts provide them but they're often shared. Bringing your own is more hygienic and fits better
  • Dry bag (10–20L) — Protects phone, wallet, and camera during boat transfers and excursions
  • Waterproof phone case — For underwater photos. The clarity is Instagram-gold
  • Rash guard — Protects from sun during long snorkel sessions. Way better than reapplying sunscreen every hour
  • GoPro or underwater camera — You'll want to capture the marine life (sea turtles, manta rays, reef sharks)

Toiletries & Health

  • Reef-safe sunscreen SPF 50+ — Regular sunscreen damages coral. Many resorts now require reef-safe products
  • After-sun/aloe vera — The equatorial sun is intense even on cloudy days
  • Mosquito repellent — Islands have mosquitoes, especially at dusk
  • Seasickness medicine — If taking speedboat transfers (common for guesthouse stays). Meclizine or Avomine work well
  • Basic medicines — Paracetamol, ORS, Imodium. Medical facilities on remote islands are limited

🎯 Must-Do Experiences

  • Snorkel with manta rays — Hanifaru Bay (Baa Atoll) is the best spot, June–November
  • Night snorkeling — See bioluminescent plankton glowing in the water
  • Sandbank picnic — Private sandbank excursions are the most Maldivian experience possible
  • Visit Malé — Walk through the fish market, Sultan Park, and Hukuru Miskiy (oldest mosque)
  • Dolphin cruise — Sunset dolphin watching trips run from most islands (₹1,500–3,000)
  • Diving — If certified, the Maldives has some of the world's best dive sites. Even beginners can do discovery dives

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Indians need a visa for Maldives?
No, Indians get a free visa on arrival in the Maldives, valid for 30 days. You need a passport valid for at least 6 months, a confirmed return ticket, and a hotel or guesthouse booking. No advance visa application is needed, making the Maldives one of the easiest international destinations from India.
What should I pack for Maldives from India?
Pack swimwear, lightweight summer clothes, reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+), a snorkel set (optional — resorts provide them), a waterproof phone pouch, a light cover-up for island walks on local islands (modest dress is expected), water shoes for coral beaches, and a GoPro or underwater camera.
What is the best time to visit Maldives from India?
November to April (dry season) is the best time — sunny skies, calm seas, and great visibility for snorkeling and diving. December-March is peak season with highest prices. May to October (wet season) sees more rain but also 30-50% lower prices and good surfing conditions on the eastern atolls.
How much does a Maldives trip cost from India?
A 4-night Maldives trip from India costs ₹40,000-70,000 per person for a guesthouse on a local island, ₹1,00,000-2,50,000 for a mid-range resort, and ₹3,00,000+ for luxury overwater villas. Direct flights from India cost ₹10,000-25,000. Local island guesthouses have made the Maldives much more affordable.

Getting Around the Maldives

Inter-island transport in the Maldives is by speedboat, seaplane, or domestic flight. Most resorts arrange airport transfers directly.

Tip: Seaplanes operate only during daylight hours (6am–4pm). If your flight arrives late, you'll need to stay overnight in Malé before transferring to your resort.
