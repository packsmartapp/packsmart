Dubai Travel & Packing Guide 2026: Complete Checklist for Indian Travelers

March 4, 2026 · 15 min read

Share this guide ✨

Dubai is the most popular international destination for Indian travelers — and it's easy to see why. Just 3–4 hours from major Indian cities, a dazzling skyline, tax-free shopping, desert adventures, and a massive Indian expat community that makes you feel right at home. Whether you're planning a family holiday, a honeymoon, a shopping spree, or a quick business trip, this guide covers everything you need to pack and prepare.

This guide is specifically designed for Indian passport holders traveling to Dubai, covering the visa process, cultural dress codes, money tips, and what you actually need versus what you can skip.

📋 Visa & Documents for Indians

Indian citizens need a pre-approved visa to enter Dubai (UAE). There's no visa on arrival for regular Indian passports, but the process is straightforward and mostly online.

Visa TypeDurationApproximate Cost

60-Day Tourist VisaExtended stay₹13,000–15,000

💡 Pro Tip: If you hold a valid US, UK, or EU visa/green card, you may be eligible for a 14-day visa on arrival at Dubai airport for AED 100 (~₹2,300). Check your eligibility on the Emirates or ICP portal before traveling.

Required Documents

  • Valid Indian passport — Minimum 6 months validity from travel date
  • Recent passport-size photo — White background
  • Confirmed return flight ticket
  • Hotel booking confirmation
  • Bank statement — Last 3–6 months (some agents request this)
  • Travel insurance — Recommended but not mandatory
⚠️ Important: Apply through authorized agents, airline websites (Emirates, Etihad, Air India), or the ICP portal (icp.gov.ae). Processing takes 3–5 working days. During peak seasons (Diwali, Christmas, New Year), apply at least 2 weeks early.

🌦️ Dubai Weather: When to Go & What to Pack

Dubai has two seasons: blisteringly hot (May–September) and pleasantly warm (October–April). The best time to visit is November through March when temperatures are comfortable for outdoor activities.

👚 The Complete Dubai Packing List

Clothing

  • 5–6 smart-casual tops — Dubai is stylish. Polo shirts, blouses, and nice t-shirts work everywhere
  • 2–3 pairs of pants/jeans — For malls, restaurants, and evening outings
  • 1–2 shorts — For beach clubs and water parks only. Avoid wearing shorts in malls and public areas
  • 1 swimsuit — For hotel pools, beaches, and water parks
  • 1 light cardigan or shawl — Malls and metro are heavily air-conditioned (often 18–20°C inside)
  • Comfortable walking shoes — You'll walk a lot in malls and souks
  • 1 dressy outfit — For fine dining, brunches, and nightlife. Dubai dress codes can be strict at upscale venues
  • Sandals or slip-ons — For beach and casual outings
⚠️ Dress Code Note: Dubai is cosmopolitan but still follows Islamic cultural norms. In malls, public spaces, and especially mosques, dress modestly — shoulders and knees should be covered. Beachwear is only appropriate at beaches and pools. The Grand Mosque requires women to wear an abaya (provided at entry).

Desert Safari Essentials

  • Closed-toe shoes — Sand gets everywhere in open sandals
  • Light long-sleeve shirt — Protects from sun and sand
  • Sunglasses — Essential for desert glare
  • Scarf or bandana — For sand protection during dune bashing
  • Light jacket — Desert evenings can drop to 15–18°C in winter

Toiletries & Health

  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ — The UV index in Dubai is extreme even in winter
  • Lip balm with SPF — Dry desert air cracks lips fast
  • Moisturizer — The combination of AC indoors and dry heat outdoors destroys your skin
  • Basic medicines — Paracetamol, antacids, antihistamines. Pharmacies are everywhere but familiar Indian brands may not be available
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Reusable water bottle — Tap water is safe to drink in Dubai (desalinated), but most people prefer bottled
💡 Medication Warning: Dubai has strict rules on medicines. Codeine, tramadol, and some common cold medicines are banned. If you carry prescription medicines, bring a doctor's letter with the prescription. Check the UAE Ministry of Health list before packing any medication.

Electronics & Tech

  • Power adapter — Dubai uses Type G plugs (same as UK — three rectangular pins). Indian plugs won't fit
  • Power bank — Long days out mean low battery
  • Phone with Nol card app — For metro/bus payments (or buy a physical Nol card)

Money & Payments

  • Forex card loaded with AED or USD — Wise, Niyo, or BookMyForex. Cards are accepted almost everywhere
  • Some AED cash — For souks, street food, taxis, and smaller shops. Carry AED 500–1,000 (~₹11,500–23,000)
  • Credit card — Visa/Mastercard widely accepted. Inform your bank about international usage
💡 Money Tip: Exchange rates at Dubai airport are poor. Either load a forex card in India, or use UAE Exchange or Al Ansari Exchange outlets in malls — they offer much better rates. ATMs charge AED 10–20 per withdrawal.

🌍 What NOT to Pack (Buy in Dubai Instead)

  • Perfumes — Dubai has incredible perfume souks with prices 30–50% cheaper than India
  • Electronics accessories — Cables, cases, power banks available cheaply at Dragon Mart
  • Sunglasses — Wide selection at souks and malls
  • Gold jewelry — Dubai Gold Souk offers some of the best prices globally (no making charges on many items)

🛃 Customs Rules: What You Can & Can't Bring

Allowed (Duty-Free)

  • 4 liters of alcohol OR 2 cartons (400 cigarettes)
  • Personal electronics
  • Gifts up to AED 3,000 in value
  • Prescription medicines with doctor's letter

Prohibited/Restricted

  • Drugs — UAE has zero-tolerance drug laws with severe penalties including imprisonment
  • Pork products — Restricted at customs
  • Vapes with nicotine — Regulated. Carry original packaging and receipts
  • Certain medications — Codeine, tramadol, and psychotropic substances are controlled. Check before packing
  • Printed/digital material — Content deemed offensive to Islamic values can be confiscated

🎯 Cultural Etiquette for Indian Travelers

  • Ramadan awareness — If visiting during Ramadan, eating, drinking, and smoking in public during daylight hours is prohibited. Restaurants have screened-off sections for non-fasting guests
  • Public displays of affection — Holding hands is generally fine for married couples, but kissing in public can lead to fines
  • Photography — Don't photograph government buildings, military areas, or people (especially women) without permission
  • Alcohol — Available at licensed restaurants, hotels, and bars. Public intoxication is a criminal offense
  • Language — Arabic is official, but English is spoken everywhere. Hindi/Urdu is widely understood thanks to the large Indian community
  • Tipping — 10–15% at restaurants (often included as "service charge"). AED 5–10 for taxi drivers and delivery personnel

📱 Essential Apps for Dubai

  • RTA (S'hail) — Official Dubai transport app for metro, bus, and water taxi
  • Careem / Uber — For taxi rides (Careem is more popular locally)
  • Zomato / Talabat — Food delivery. Zomato works in Dubai and you'll find Indian restaurants everywhere
  • Google Maps — Download Dubai offline maps
  • Visit Dubai — Official tourism app with deals, events, and attraction info
  • XE Currency — Quick INR to AED conversions

🏖️ Top Experiences You Shouldn't Miss

  • Burj Khalifa — Book "At the Top" tickets online (₹3,000–5,000). Sunset slot is the most popular
  • Desert Safari — Dune bashing, camel rides, BBQ dinner, and belly dancing (₹2,500–5,000 per person)
  • Dubai Mall + Fountain Show — Free fountain shows every 30 minutes after 6 PM
  • Gold & Spice Souks in Deira — Take the Abra (water taxi) for AED 1 across Dubai Creek
  • Global Village — Open Oct–Apr. Shopping, food, and cultural pavilions from 90+ countries (India pavilion is massive)
  • Miracle Garden — World's largest natural flower garden. Open Nov–May
  • IMG Worlds of Adventure / Aquaventure — For families and thrill-seekers

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Indians need a visa for Dubai?
Yes, Indians need a visa to visit Dubai (UAE). You can apply for an e-visa online, a 14-day or 30-day tourist visa through airlines like Emirates, or through approved travel agencies. Processing takes 3-5 working days. A 30-day tourist visa costs approximately ₹5,000-8,000 depending on the provider.
What should I pack for Dubai from India?
Pack modest, lightweight clothing — shoulders and knees should be covered in malls and public areas. Bring comfortable walking shoes for desert activities, formal wear for brunches and nightclubs, a light jacket for aggressively air-conditioned malls, swimwear for beaches and water parks, sunscreen (SPF 50+), and sunglasses. Dubai uses Type G (UK) power outlets.
What is the best time to visit Dubai from India?
November to March is the best time — pleasant temperatures of 20-30°C, ideal for outdoor activities and desert safaris. Avoid June to September when temperatures exceed 45°C. December-January is peak tourist season with events like Dubai Shopping Festival. April-May and October are shoulder seasons with good deals.
How much does a Dubai trip cost from India?
A 5-day Dubai trip costs approximately ₹40,000-70,000 per person on a budget, ₹70,000-1,50,000 for mid-range, and ₹1,50,000+ for luxury. Return flights from India range from ₹12,000-25,000. Dubai has many free attractions — beaches, Dubai Mall, fountain shows, and souks — that help keep costs down.
