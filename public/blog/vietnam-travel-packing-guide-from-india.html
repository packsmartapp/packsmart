Vietnam Travel & Packing Guide 2026: Complete Checklist for Indian Travelers

March 4, 2026 · 15 min read

Share this guide: WhatsApp Twitter Facebook LinkedIn

Vietnam is rapidly becoming one of the hottest destinations for Indian travelers — and for good reason. Stunning landscapes from Ha Long Bay to the Mekong Delta, some of the best street food in Asia, fascinating history, and prices that make even budget travelers feel rich. A bowl of pho for ₹100, a hotel room for ₹1,500, and experiences that rival destinations costing 5x more.

This guide covers everything Indian travelers need to know — from the e-visa process to packing for Vietnam's wildly different climate zones, street food safety, and navigating the organized chaos of Vietnamese traffic.

📋 Visa & Documents for Indians

Indian citizens need a visa to enter Vietnam. The easiest option is the e-visa, which you can apply for entirely online.

Visa TypeDurationCost
E-Visa (Single Entry)Up to 90 days$25 (~₹2,100)
E-Visa (Multiple Entry)Up to 90 days$50 (~₹4,200)
Visa on ArrivalUp to 30 days$25 + approval letter fee
💡 Pro Tip: Apply for the e-visa at the official portal: evisa.gov.vn. Processing takes 3–7 business days. The e-visa is linked to your passport — print it and carry it. Avoid third-party sites that charge extra.

Required Documents

  • Passport — Valid for 6+ months with 2 blank pages
  • Passport photo — 4x6cm, white background
  • Return flight confirmation
  • Hotel booking — At least the first few nights

🌦️ Vietnam Weather by Region

Vietnam stretches 1,650 km north to south, so the weather varies dramatically by region. This is crucial for packing.

Typhoon season Sep–Nov. Dry Feb–Aug

Hot year-round. Wet May–Nov, dry Dec–Apr

⚠️ Packing Tip: If you're traveling north to south (the classic route: Hanoi → Ha Long Bay → Hoi An → Ho Chi Minh City), you'll need both warm and tropical clothing. Layer up for the north, strip down for the south.

👚 The Complete Vietnam Packing List

Clothing

  • 5–6 lightweight tops — Quick-dry, breathable. You'll sweat in the south and may get caught in rain
  • 2–3 shorts — For southern and central Vietnam
  • 2 long pants — For Hanoi (cooler weather), temple visits, and motorbike rides (protects legs)
  • 1 warm fleece/hoodie — Essential if visiting Sapa or Hanoi in winter (Dec–Feb). Nights can drop to 8–10°C
  • Rain jacket — Compact, packable. Rain hits fast and hard, especially in central Vietnam
  • Comfortable walking shoes — Waterproof or quick-dry. Sidewalks are uneven and often wet
  • Flip-flops — For beach towns and casual use
  • Modest clothing for temples — Shoulders and knees covered at pagodas and temples

Street Food Survival Kit

  • Hand sanitizer — Not all street stalls have handwashing facilities
  • Tissue/wet wipes — Many small restaurants don't provide napkins (or charge for them)
  • ORS packets from India (Electral) — For dehydration from heat or traveler's stomach
  • Imodium/Loperamide — Just in case. Vietnamese street food is generally safe if you follow the locals, but adjustment takes a day or two
  • Antacids — Vietnamese food can be spicy and heavy on fish sauce for sensitive stomachs
💡 Street Food Rule: Eat where locals eat. If a stall has a queue of Vietnamese people, the food is fresh and safe. Avoid stalls with pre-cooked food sitting in the sun. The best pho, banh mi, and bun cha come from tiny stalls with plastic stools on the sidewalk.

Electronics & Tech

  • Power adapter — Vietnam uses Type A, C, and F plugs. Most Indian chargers (Type C round pins) work in many outlets, but carry a universal adapter to be safe
  • Power bank (20,000mAh) — Long bus rides between cities drain your phone
  • Offline Google Maps — Download Hanoi, Hoi An, and HCMC offline. Data can be spotty in rural areas

Money & Payments

  • USD cash ($100–200) — For emergency and better exchange rates
  • Forex card — ATMs are widespread. Withdraw Vietnamese Dong (VND) on arrival
  • Small VND denominations — Street vendors often can't break large bills (500,000 VND). Keep 10,000–50,000 notes handy
⚠️ Currency Confusion: Vietnamese Dong has many zeros. 1 USD ≈ 25,500 VND. A meal might cost 50,000 VND (~₹170). Double-check zeros when paying — the difference between 50,000 and 500,000 is easy to miss. Count your change carefully.

📱 Essential Apps

  • Grab — Uber of Southeast Asia. Works in all major cities for rides and food
  • Google Maps — Download offline maps for each city
  • 12Go Asia — Book buses, trains, and flights between cities
  • Google Translate — Download Vietnamese offline. Very few people speak English outside tourist areas
  • XE Currency — INR to VND (you'll need it with all those zeros)

🎯 Must-Do Experiences

  • Ha Long Bay cruise — Overnight on a junk boat among limestone karsts. ₹5,000–15,000 for a 2-day/1-night cruise
  • Hanoi Old Quarter — Walk the 36 streets, eat egg coffee, try bun cha (Obama's meal), and watch morning tai chi at Hoan Kiem Lake
  • Hoi An Ancient Town — Lantern-lit streets, custom tailoring (get a suit made in 24 hours for ₹3,000–5,000), and the best banh mi in Vietnam
  • Cu Chi Tunnels — Underground tunnel network near HCMC. Fascinating war history
  • Mekong Delta — Floating markets, coconut candy workshops, and river boat tours
  • Sapa rice terraces — Trekking through mountain villages and terraced rice fields
  • Phong Nha caves — Home to the world's largest cave (Son Doong) and stunning Phong Nha cave

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Indians need a visa for Vietnam?
Yes, Indians need a visa for Vietnam. The easiest option is the e-visa, which costs $25 USD, takes 3 working days to process, and is valid for 90 days (single or multiple entry). Apply online at the official Vietnam Immigration portal. You can also get a visa on arrival with a pre-approved letter.
What should I pack for Vietnam from India?
Pack lightweight, modest clothes, a light rain jacket, comfortable walking shoes for city streets, a money belt (petty theft can occur), mosquito repellent, sunscreen, a hat, a quick-dry towel, and a universal power adapter (Type A/C/G outlets). Bring layers if visiting the north during winter (Hanoi can drop to 10°C in January).
What is the best time to visit Vietnam from India?
It depends on the region. For the whole country, March-April and September-November are best. Northern Vietnam (Hanoi, Ha Long Bay) is best October-April. Central Vietnam (Da Nang, Hoi An) is best February-August. Southern Vietnam (Ho Chi Minh City) is best December-April. Vietnam spans 1,650 km, so weather varies significantly.
How much does a Vietnam trip cost from India?
A 10-day Vietnam trip costs approximately ₹30,000-50,000 per person on a budget, ₹50,000-1,00,000 mid-range, and ₹1,00,000+ luxury. Vietnam is one of the cheapest destinations in Asia. Street food costs ₹50-150 per meal, budget hotels ₹500-1,500/night. Flights from India cost ₹15,000-30,000 with one stop (no direct flights).
