1. Visa & Entry Requirements for Indian Citizens

Unlike many countries, Indians cannot use ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization). You must obtain a B1/B2 Tourist Visa before traveling to the USA.

B1/B2 Tourist Visa Process:

  • Form DS-160: Complete online at travel.state.gov
  • Interview: Schedule at US Embassy/Consulate in India (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata)
  • Documents Required: Passport, appointment confirmation, DS-160 confirmation, financial proof (bank statements), hotel/itinerary bookings
  • Fee: $185 (~₹15,500 at current rates)
  • Processing Time: 2-6 weeks (can vary seasonally)
  • Validity: Usually 10 years for Indian citizens

5. What NOT to Pack - USA Customs Restrictions

⚠️ Strict US Customs Rules

USA has strict customs regulations. Violations can result in confiscation, fines, or serious issues with your visa.

Do NOT Pack:

  • Food items with meat/dairy: No samosas with meat, paneer items, meat curry, dried fish. Indian spices are OK.
  • Plants, seeds, or fresh produce: No dried mango slices, seeds, or plant material
  • Weapons: Knives, switchblades (even small ones), pepper spray illegal in some states
  • Medications in bulk: Large quantities of prescription drugs look suspicious. Pack only what you need + small buffer (30-day supply OK).
  • Cash exceeding $10,000: Must declare if carrying more (no legal issue if declared)
  • Batteries in check baggage: Lithium batteries must follow airline rules
  • Chargers exceeding airline limits
  • Items resembling weapons

What's OK to Pack:

  • Spices and masalas (turmeric, cumin, coriander, etc.)
  • Tea leaves, coffee beans
  • Dry snacks without meat (moong namkeen, chikhalwali, dry fruits)
  • Sweets without dairy (dry dates, raisins, nuts)
  • Medications (with doctor's letter, original bottles)
  • Religious items

6. USA Cultural Etiquette for Indian Travelers

Tipping Culture (IMPORTANT!)

Tipping is NOT optional in the USA. It's expected and staff depend on tips for income. Here's the breakdown:

  • Restaurants (dine-in): 15-20% of bill. Add tip at card machine.
  • Restaurants (takeout): 10% or round up
  • Taxis/Uber/Lyft: 15-20%
  • Hotel staff: $2-5 per night for housekeeping
  • Baggage handlers: $2 per bag
  • Tour guides: 15-20%
  • Haircut/salon: 15-20%
  • Bartenders: $1-2 per drink

Other Etiquette Tips:

  • Personal Space: Americans maintain 1.5-2 feet distance. Don't stand too close.
  • Small Talk: "How are you?" is common greeting. Respond briefly and ask back. Small talk is normal before transactions.
  • Sales Tax: Prices shown in stores DON'T include tax (7-10%). The final bill will be higher.
  • Queuing: People stand in line. Always line up, don't cut queues.
  • Driving Rules: Drive on the RIGHT side. This is a huge adjustment for Indians! Stop signs are absolute. Speed limits are enforced.
  • Public Display of Affection: More acceptable than in India, but still be respectful.
  • Business Casual: Dress code for business is less formal than India. Business casual is standard.
  • Directness: Americans are direct. "No" means no. Politeness is still valued but doesn't mean the same as in India.

7. Complete Budget Guide for Indian Travelers

The USA is expensive compared to India. Here's a realistic breakdown in Indian Rupees:

Major Expenses (10-day trip)

  • International Flights (round-trip): ₹50,000 - ₹1,20,000 (economy, varies by city/season)
  • Hotels (mid-range): ₹5,000 - ₹15,000 per night = ₹50,000 - ₹1,50,000 total
  • Food (moderate): ₹2,000 - ₹5,000 per day = ₹20,000 - ₹50,000 total
  • Transportation (local): ₹500 - ₹2,000 per day = ₹5,000 - ₹20,000 total
  • Activities/Attractions: ₹10,000 - ₹50,000 (varies widely)
  • Travel Insurance: ₹3,000 - ₹8,000
  • Visa Fee: ₹15,500 (one-time)

Total 10-Day Trip Budget (per person)

  • Budget Trip: ₹2,00,000 - ₹2,50,000 (budget hotels, cheap eats)
  • Mid-Range Trip: ₹3,00,000 - ₹4,00,000 (decent hotels, good restaurants, activities)
  • Luxury Trip: ₹5,00,000+ (5-star hotels, high-end restaurants, premium experiences)

Money-Saving Tips:

  • Visit Free Attractions: Statue of Liberty Grounds (free), Brooklyn Bridge (free walk), Central Park (free), many museums have "pay what you wish" hours
  • Use Public Transport: NYC MetroCard, LA Metro, BART in SF - much cheaper than Uber for multiple trips
  • Eat like Indians: Indian restaurants are affordable (₹500-1,500 for meal) and found in every major city
  • Happy Hour: Many restaurants have 4-7 PM happy hour discounts
  • Grocery Shopping: Buy breakfast items, snacks from grocery stores (Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, Safeway)
  • Travel in Groups: Share hotel rooms, rent vehicles together, book group tours
  • Book Accommodations Early: Advance bookings are cheaper
  • Visit during Off-Season: Spring and Fall are cheaper than Summer/Winter holidays

10. Popular Destinations: City & Regional Tips

New York City

  • Indian Neighborhoods: Jackson Heights, Murray Hill ("Curry Hill"), Flushing (Queens)
  • Must-Visit: Statue of Liberty, Central Park, Times Square, Metropolitan Museum
  • Indian Food: Incredible options in Jackson Heights. Try Baluchi's, Dhaba, Haandi
  • Bollywood: NYC has massive Indian film industry presence. Catch Indian movies at AMC theaters.
  • Transport: MTA subway is efficient. Buy MetroCard for multiple days.
  • Accommodation: Hotels ₹8,000-20,000/night. Airbnb in Queens cheaper.

Los Angeles & Hollywood

  • Must-Visit: Hollywood Walk of Fame, Griffith Observatory, Santa Monica Pier, Getty Center
  • Bollywood Connection: Large Indian population in West Hollywood and Koreatown
  • Driving: LA requires a car. Rent one; public transport is limited.
  • Beaches: Venice Beach, Santa Monica, Malibu - free beach access
  • Budget: ₹5,000-12,000/night hotels, food ₹2,000-4,000/day

San Francisco & Bay Area

  • Tech Hub: Home to Indian-origin tech CEOs and engineers (Sundar Pichai, Satya Nadella)
  • Must-Visit: Golden Gate Bridge, Cable Cars, Painted Ladies, Alcatraz
  • Indian Community: Strong presence in Fremont, San Jose (tech hub)
  • Transport: BART (Bay Area Rapid Transit) excellent public transport
  • Weather: Cool year-round (15-20°C). Bring jacket always.
  • Expensive: ₹10,000-18,000/night, food ₹2,500-5,000/day

Las Vegas

  • Must-Visit: The Strip, Bellagio Fountains, Grand Canyon day trips
  • Affordable: Cheap hotels (₹3,000-8,000), free entertainment
  • Gambling: Most casinos are FREE to enter and watch. Try your luck at slot machines or tables.
  • Indian Population: Growing community, some Indian restaurants
  • Climate: Extremely hot in summer (40°C+). Desert conditions.

Miami & South Florida

  • Weather: Warm year-round (22-30°C). Perfect for beach lovers.
  • Must-Visit: South Beach, Art Deco District, Everglades, Key West
  • Cuban Culture: Strong Hispanic influence. Try Cuban food.
  • Accommodation: ₹5,000-15,000/night depending on location
  • Hurricane Season: Jun-Nov has risk. Travel insurance essential.

Washington DC

  • Must-Visit: White House, Capitol, Smithsonian Museums (all FREE!), Lincoln Memorial
  • Budget-Friendly: Most major attractions are free government museums
  • Walkable: Good for walking tours
  • Accommodation: ₹5,000-12,000/night
  • Best Season: Spring (cherry blossoms) and Fall

Grand Canyon & Natural Wonders

  • Must-Do: Grand Canyon rim walk, hiking, sunrise viewing
  • Where: Arizona, Utah, Colorado
  • Driving: Rent a car. Long distances between attractions.
  • Climate: Very hot (40°C+) in summer. Cold at night.
  • Tour Options: GetYourGuide offers Grand Canyon tours →

14. Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: Do Indians need a visa for USA?

Yes, Indians require a B1/B2 Tourist Visa. Unlike many nationalities, ESTA does NOT apply to Indian passport holders. You must apply at the US Embassy/Consulate in India. The process involves:

  • Online DS-160 form completion
  • In-person interview at the embassy
  • Fee of $185 (~₹15,500)
  • Processing time of 2-6 weeks
  • Visa validity typically 10 years

Q2: What should I pack for USA from India?

Packing depends on season and region, but key items include:

  • Comfortable walking shoes (you'll walk a lot)
  • Voltage converter (USA is 110V, India is 230V)
  • Power adapters (Type A/B plugs)
  • Casual clothing (Americans dress informally)
  • Medications in original bottles
  • Travel insurance documentation
  • Season-appropriate clothing (see section 3 for details)
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
DO NOT pack: food with meat/dairy, plants, weapons, excessive medications.

Q3: What's the best time to visit USA from India?

It depends on your preferences:

  • Spring (Mar-May): Ideal nationwide. Pleasant weather, fewer crowds, moderate prices.
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): Hot but great for beaches, world cup 2026 (Jun-Jul), peak season means higher prices.
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): Excellent overall. Fall foliage, fewer crowds, lower prices.
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Cold in Northeast, ideal in South (Florida, Arizona), good for skiing.
If attending FIFA World Cup 2026, you must travel Jun-Jul 2026.

Q4: How much does a USA trip cost from India?

Budget for a 10-day trip per person:

  • Budget Trip: ₹2,00,000-2,50,000 (budget hotels, local food, free attractions)
  • Mid-Range Trip: ₹3,00,000-4,00,000 (decent hotels, good restaurants, paid activities)
  • Luxury Trip: ₹5,00,000+ (5-star hotels, fine dining, premium experiences)
Major costs: Flights (₹50,000-1,20,000), Hotels (₹5,000-15,000/night), Food (₹2,000-5,000/day), Activities (varies). Travel insurance is critical for USA due to expensive healthcare.
