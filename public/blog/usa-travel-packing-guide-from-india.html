1. Visa & Entry Requirements

Visa requirements vary by nationality:

  • ESTA Eligible: UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, most EU nations, and other Visa Waiver Program countries can use ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization). Cost: $21 online. Apply at least 72 hours before travel.
  • B-1/B-2 Visa Required: India, China, Russia, Brazil, and many other countries require a B-1/B-2 Tourist Visa before traveling to the USA.

B1/B2 Tourist Visa Process:

  • Form DS-160: Complete online at travel.state.gov
  • Interview: Schedule at US Embassy/Consulate in your country (find your nearest embassy at travel.state.gov)
  • Documents Required: Passport, appointment confirmation, DS-160 confirmation, financial proof (bank statements), hotel/itinerary bookings
  • Fee: $185 USD
  • Processing Time: 2-6 weeks (can vary seasonally)
  • Validity: Typically 10 years (varies by nationality)

  • Sunscreen (SPF 50+)

Fall (Sep-Nov) Packing List

Winter (Dec-Feb) Packing List

  • Thick socks

  • Phone charger (USB-C recommended)
  • Power bank (for long day trips)

  • Sunscreen (SPF 50+)

  • Face wash
  • Toothbrush & paste

Other Essentials

5. What NOT to Pack - USA Customs Restrictions

⚠️ Strict US Customs Rules

USA has strict customs regulations. Violations can result in confiscation, fines, or serious issues with your visa.

Do NOT Pack:

  • Food items with meat/dairy: No meat products, cheese, dairy items, or seafood. Spices and dried herbs are generally OK.
  • Plants, seeds, or fresh produce: No dried mango slices, seeds, or plant material
  • Weapons: Knives, switchblades (even small ones), pepper spray illegal in some states
  • Medications in bulk: Large quantities of prescription drugs look suspicious. Pack only what you need + small buffer (30-day supply OK).
  • Cash exceeding $10,000: Must declare if carrying more (no legal issue if declared)
  • Batteries in check baggage: Lithium batteries must follow airline rules
  • Chargers exceeding airline limits
  • Items resembling weapons

What's OK to Pack:

  • Spices and masalas (turmeric, cumin, coriander, etc.)
  • Tea leaves, coffee beans
  • Dry snacks without meat (moong namkeen, chikhalwali, dry fruits)
  • Sweets without dairy (dry dates, raisins, nuts)
  • Medications (with doctor's letter, original bottles)
  • Religious items

6. USA Cultural Etiquette

Tipping Culture (IMPORTANT!)

Tipping is NOT optional in the USA. It's expected and staff depend on tips for income. Here's the breakdown:

  • Restaurants (dine-in): 15-20% of bill. Add tip at card machine.
  • Restaurants (takeout): 10% or round up
  • Taxis/Uber/Lyft: 15-20%
  • Hotel staff: $2-5 per night for housekeeping
  • Baggage handlers: $2 per bag
  • Tour guides: 15-20%
  • Haircut/salon: 15-20%
  • Bartenders: $1-2 per drink

Other Etiquette Tips:

  • Personal Space: Americans maintain 1.5-2 feet distance. Don't stand too close.
  • Small Talk: "How are you?" is common greeting. Respond briefly and ask back. Small talk is normal before transactions.
  • Sales Tax: Prices shown in stores DON'T include tax (7-10%). The final bill will be higher.
  • Queuing: People stand in line. Always line up, don't cut queues.
  • Driving Rules: Drive on the RIGHT side of the road. Stop signs are absolute and enforceable. Speed limits are strictly enforced.
  • Public Display of Affection: More liberal than in many countries, but still be respectful of others.
  • Business Casual: Dress code for business is typically casual to business casual. Standards vary by company and location.
  • Directness: Americans tend to be direct and straightforward. "No" means no. Politeness is valued but differs from many cultures.

7. Complete Budget Guide

The USA is an expensive destination. Here's a realistic breakdown in USD for a 10-day trip:

Major Expenses (10-day trip)

  • International Flights (round-trip): $400 - $800 (economy, varies by origin/season)
  • Hotels (mid-range): $120 - $200 per night = $1,200 - $2,000 total
  • Food (moderate): $35 - $65 per day = $350 - $650 total
  • Transportation (local): $30 - $100 per day = $300 - $1,000 total
  • Activities/Attractions: $200 - $500 (varies widely)
  • Travel Insurance: $50 - $150
  • Visa Fee: $185 (one-time, if required)

Total 10-Day Trip Budget (per person)

  • Budget Trip: $800 - $1,000 (budget hotels, casual dining)
  • Mid-Range Trip: $1,200 - $1,600 (decent hotels, good restaurants, activities)
  • Luxury Trip: $2,000+ (5-star hotels, high-end restaurants, premium experiences)

Money-Saving Tips:

  • Visit Free Attractions: Statue of Liberty Grounds (free), Brooklyn Bridge (free walk), Central Park (free), many museums have "pay what you wish" hours
  • Use Public Transport: NYC MetroCard, LA Metro, BART in SF - much cheaper than Uber for multiple trips
  • International Cuisine: Affordable restaurants from around the world are found in every major city (budget meals $8-20)
  • Happy Hour: Many restaurants have 4-7 PM happy hour discounts
  • Grocery Shopping: Buy breakfast items, snacks from grocery stores (Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, Safeway)
  • Travel in Groups: Share hotel rooms, rent vehicles together, book group tours
  • Book Accommodations Early: Advance bookings are cheaper
  • Visit during Off-Season: Spring and Fall are cheaper than Summer/Winter holidays

10. Popular Destinations: City & Regional Tips

New York City

  • Diverse Neighborhoods: Jackson Heights, Murray Hill, Flushing (Queens) — multicultural dining and entertainment
  • Must-Visit: Statue of Liberty, Central Park, Times Square, Metropolitan Museum — book skip-the-line tickets to save time at popular attractions
  • International Cuisine: World-class dining from every country. Jackson Heights has excellent Asian restaurants.
  • Entertainment: World-class theaters and cultural centers. Check AMC and independent theaters for international cinema.
  • Transport: MTA subway is efficient. Buy MetroCard for multiple days.
  • Accommodation: Hotels $120-240/night. Airbnb in Queens cheaper.

Los Angeles & Hollywood

  • Must-Visit: Hollywood Walk of Fame, Griffith Observatory, Santa Monica Pier, Getty Center
  • Diverse Communities: LA has large communities from around the world, including West Hollywood and Koreatown
  • Driving: LA requires a car. Rent one; public transport is limited.
  • Beaches: Venice Beach, Santa Monica, Malibu - free beach access
  • Budget: $80-150/night hotels, food $40-60/day

San Francisco & Bay Area

  • Tech Hub: Home to major tech companies and talented engineers from around the world
  • Must-Visit: Golden Gate Bridge, Cable Cars, Painted Ladies, Alcatraz
  • Diverse Communities: Multicultural population in Fremont, San Jose (tech hub)
  • Transport: BART (Bay Area Rapid Transit) excellent public transport
  • Weather: Cool year-round (60-70°F). Bring jacket always.
  • Expensive: $150-220/night hotels, food $30-60/day

Las Vegas

  • Must-Visit: The Strip, Bellagio Fountains, Grand Canyon day trips
  • Affordable: Budget hotels ($50-120), free entertainment
  • Gambling: Most casinos are FREE to enter and watch. Try your luck at slot machines or tables.
  • Diverse Communities: Growing multicultural population with international dining options
  • Climate: Extremely hot in summer (105-115°F). Desert conditions.

Miami & South Florida

  • Weather: Warm year-round (22-30°C). Perfect for beach lovers.
  • Must-Visit: South Beach, Art Deco District, Everglades, Key West
  • Cuban Culture: Strong Hispanic influence. Try Cuban food.
  • Accommodation: $80-200/night depending on location
  • Hurricane Season: Jun-Nov has risk. Travel insurance essential.

Washington DC

  • Must-Visit: White House, Capitol, Smithsonian Museums (all FREE!), Lincoln Memorial
  • Budget-Friendly: Most major attractions are free government museums
  • Walkable: Good for walking tours
  • Accommodation: $80-150/night
  • Best Season: Spring (cherry blossoms) and Fall

Grand Canyon & Natural Wonders

  • Must-Do: Grand Canyon rim walk, hiking, sunrise viewing
  • Where: Arizona, Utah, Colorado
  • Driving: Rent a car. Long distances between attractions.
  • Climate: Very hot (40°C+) in summer. Cold at night.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need travel insurance for USA?
Absolutely yes. USA healthcare is among the world's most expensive. A broken bone can cost $10,000+. Travel insurance is essential and should cover medical emergencies, evacuation, and trip cancellation. SafetyWing or your home country's insurance provider can help.
Can I work in the USA as a tourist?
No. A B1/B2 tourist visa does not permit work. You cannot freelance, do paid internships, or take any employment. If you plan to work, you need an H1B or other work visa.
How much cash should I carry to USA?
Credit cards and debit cards are preferred. Carry $200-400 USD for emergencies. Cards work everywhere - ATMs, restaurants, hotels. Carrying excessive cash is not recommended.
Are there direct international flights to USA?
Yes, many airlines offer international flights to major US cities. Most flights from Asia (India, China, Japan) to USA are 15-18 hours. From Europe typically 8-10 hours. Book well in advance for better prices.
