Visa & Entry Requirements for Americans, Brits, Australians & Canadians

Greece is part of the Schengen Area, making entry straightforward for citizens of the US, UK, Australia, and Canada. Here's what you need to know:

US Citizens

  • Visa: Not required for stays up to 90 days within a 180-day period
  • Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel dates
  • ETIAS: From 2025, US citizens will need to obtain an ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorization System) prior to travel
  • Cost: ETIAS will cost approximately $7 USD and is valid for 3 years

UK Citizens

  • Visa: Post-Brexit, UK citizens can stay visa-free for 90 days in any 180-day period
  • Passport: Must have at least 6 months validity remaining
  • Passport Conditions: Your passport must have been issued on or after your UK passport renewal date (no old passports)
  • ETIAS: Will be required starting 2025
  • Cost: €7 (approximately $7.50 USD)

Australian Citizens

  • Visa: Visa-free for 90 days in any 180-day period
  • Passport: Must be valid for the duration of stay
  • ETIAS: Will be mandatory from 2025
  • Cost: €7 (approximately $7.50 USD)

Canadian Citizens

  • Visa: Visa-free for 90 days in any 180-day period
  • Passport: Valid for the duration of your stay
  • ETIAS: Required from 2025
  • Cost: €7 (approximately $7.50 USD)
🎯 Pro Tip: Apply for ETIAS authorization as soon as it becomes available. While approved authorizations are usually granted within minutes, it's best to apply well before your trip. Multiple entries are allowed within the 3-year validity period.

Best Time to Visit Greece: Seasons & Island Hopping Seasons

April - May: Spring Season (Sweet Spot)

Perfect for first-time visitors and island hoppers. Temperatures range from 68-75°F (20-24°C), wildflowers are in bloom, and crowds are manageable. All ferries operate on full schedules. This is ideal for mixing beach time with hiking and cultural exploration.

June - August: Peak Summer Season

Hot and crowded, but vibrant! Temperatures soar to 85-95°F (29-35°C), water is warmest (75-80°F), and nightlife is active. Expect high prices and packed ferries. Best for beach lovers who don't mind crowds. Ferry schedules are most frequent.

September - October: Autumn Season (The Second Best Time)

Often called the "second summer," September offers warm weather (77-82°F / 25-28°C), calmer seas, and fewer tourists. October is cooler (68-73°F / 20-23°C) but still pleasant. Water is still warm enough for swimming. Great for hiking and exploration without summer crowds.

November - March: Winter Season

Quiet and budget-friendly (prices drop 30-50%), but many island services close or reduce operations. Temperatures are 50-60°F (10-15°C), rainy days are common, and water is cold. Best for experienced travelers seeking solitude or focusing on mainland exploration.

💡 Island Hopping Season Tip: May and September are perfect for island hopping—good weather, manageable crowds, and full ferry schedules. Book accommodation 2-3 weeks in advance rather than last-minute.

What to Pack for Greece: Complete Packing Guide

Summer Clothing & Footwear (April-October)

Essential Summer Items:

  • Lightweight cotton T-shirts (4-5)
  • Short-sleeve linen shirts (2)
  • Lightweight pants/long shorts (2)
  • Skirts/dresses (2-3)
  • Swimsuits (2-3)
  • Lightweight cardigan or wrap
  • Sarong or beach cover-up
  • Comfortable walking sandals (Birkenstocks, Chacos)
  • Boat shoes or water shoes (for rocky beaches)
  • Light evening shoes or dressy sandals
  • Lightweight jacket for cool evenings
  • Underwear & socks (5-7 days' worth)
  • Lightweight sleepwear

Sun & Beach Protection

  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ (broad-spectrum, water-resistant)

  • Lightweight hiking boots or trail shoes
  • Moisture-wicking hiking socks (2-3 pairs)

  • Windbreaker or light rain jacket
  • Small hiking backpack (15-20L)
  • Reusable water bottle (1L capacity minimum)
  • Electrolyte drink powder or tablets
  • Energy bars or snacks
  • Trail map or offline hiking app (AllTrails)

Technology & Electronics

Smart Traveler Tech:

  • Smartphone (with offline maps downloaded)

  • Portable power bank (20,000mAh+)
  • USB-C charging cables (2)
  • Headphones or earbuds
  • Camera (optional: GoPro for water activities)
  • Smartwatch (helpful for ferry schedules)

Toiletries & Health

Health & Personal Care Items:

  • Prescription medications (in original containers)
  • Travel-size toiletries (shampoo, conditioner, soap)

  • Deodorant

  • Insect repellent

Island vs. Mainland Packing Considerations

For Island Hopping: Pack lighter—aim for 15kg (33lbs) maximum. Multiple ferries mean multiple opportunities to lose luggage. Keep valuables in a day pack. Consider compression cubes to maximize space. Travel with a roller bag AND a backpack for flexibility.

For Mainland (Athens, Delphi): You can pack slightly more comfortably as you'll be based in one location. Still prioritize comfortable walking shoes as cities require extensive sightseeing on foot. Athens and Delphi have cobblestone streets that are challenging for rolling luggage.

📦 Packing Pro Tip: Leave 20-30% of luggage space empty for souvenirs and gifts. Greek olive oil, feta cheese, and local crafts are must-buys. Consider purchasing items during your last few days and packing them in your checked luggage if flying home.

Island-Specific Travel Guides

Santorini: The Iconic Greek Island

Best For: Romance, stunning sunsets, wine tasting, photography

Climate: Very hot in summer (95°F+), windy

Special Packing Notes:

  • Bring comfortable walking shoes—Oia and Fira involve steep, uneven cobblestone streets and hundreds of steps
  • Windbreaker jacket (strong Etesian winds in summer)
  • Extra sunscreen (white volcanic rock reflects intense UV)
  • Water shoes for unique black-sand and red-sand beaches
  • Nice dinner outfit for sunset restaurants (casual but neat)

Activities to Prepare For: Volcano caldera hike, wine tastings, beach days, sunset viewing, scuba diving

Suggested Duration: 3-4 days minimum (2 days for quick visit)

Mykonos: Party Island & Chic Destination

Best For: Nightlife, beaches, upscale dining, LGBT-friendly travel

Climate: Hot and windy

Special Packing Notes:

  • Beach club outfits (trendy but minimal clothing for day clubs)
  • Evening wear for nightclubs (no athletic wear or flip-flops)
  • Lightweight wraps or cover-ups
  • Water shoes (pebble beaches)
  • Sunglasses (very sunny)

Budget Note: Mykonos is expensive—expect €50-80+ per meal at decent restaurants. Gyro sandwiches offer good value (€5-7).

Suggested Duration: 2-3 days

Crete: Mountains, Gorges & Ancient History

Best For: Hiking, archaeology, authentic villages, less touristy beaches

Climate: Cooler than smaller islands, more variable weather

Special Packing Notes:

  • Sturdy hiking boots (Samaria Gorge hike is world-famous)
  • Long pants for mountain hiking
  • Light jacket for mountain areas
  • Extra water bottles
  • Detailed trail maps or offline hiking apps

Must-Do: Samaria Gorge trek (7-mile hike, 4-6 hours)—requires preparation and early start.

Suggested Duration: 3-4 days (allows time for hiking and beaches)

Rhodes: Historic & Diverse

Best For: Medieval history, diverse beaches, water sports, Greek island experience

Climate: Hot and sunny, slightly less windy than northern islands

Special Packing Notes:

  • Comfortable walking shoes for Old Town exploration
  • Snorkeling gear (optional—rentals available)
  • Casual evening wear

Attractions: Palace of the Grand Masters, ancient ruins, Lindos Acropolis, pristine beaches.

Suggested Duration: 3 days

Corfu: Green & European

Best For: Less touristy experience, green landscapes, Venetian architecture

Climate: Cooler and greener (more rain than southern islands)

Special Packing Notes:

  • Light rain jacket
  • Closed-toe shoes for exploring old town
  • Binoculars for birdwatching

Why Visit: Authentic Greek island experience without extreme summer crowds. Great for families.

Suggested Duration: 2-3 days

Athens City Essentials: Capital Exploration

Athens blends ancient ruins with modern European city vibrancy. You'll be walking extensively—prioritize comfort.

Athens Packing Specifics

  • Comfortable walking shoes: The Acropolis, Plaka, Psyrri neighborhoods require hours of walking on uneven ancient streets and cobblestones
  • Light layers: Museums are heavily air-conditioned; streets are hot
  • Hat/sunglasses: Many attractions have minimal shade
  • Modest clothing: For temple visits, avoid overly revealing clothing (shoulders and knees covered is respectful)
  • Casual evening attire: Nice restaurants don't require formal dress but neat casual is standard

Must-See Attractions (Plan 2-3 days minimum)

  • Acropolis & Parthenon: Arrive early (7am opening) to avoid crowds. Book skip-the-line tickets. Budget 3-4 hours. Wear strong sunscreen.
  • Acropolis Museum: Outstanding modern museum with ancient treasures. Get tickets. 2-3 hours.
  • Temple of Olympian Zeus: Less crowded than Acropolis. 45 minutes.
  • Panathenaic Stadium: Ancient Olympic site. 30 minutes.
  • Plaka District: Charming old neighborhood with tavernas. Great for evening strolls.
  • Psyrri District: Trendy neighborhood with street art, cafes, restaurants.
  • National Archaeological Museum: World-class collection. 2-3 hours.

Food in Athens

Try authentic Greek tavernas in Plaka or Psyrri. Budget $10-20 USD for lunch, $15-30 for dinner at casual restaurants. Street food gyros are $5-7 and excellent quality.

Ferry & Island Hopping Logistics: Getting Around Greece

Main Ferry Companies

  • Hellenic Seaways: High-speed ferries, covers most major routes. Prices: €20-80 depending on distance
  • Blue Star Ferries: Car ferries and passenger ferries, overnight options available. Prices: €15-50
  • Aegean Speed Lines: Fast ferries between central islands. Prices: €25-70
  • SeaJets: Catamarans for Cyclades islands. Prices: €30-80

How to Book Ferries

Online: Book through Ferryhopper, GreekFerries.gr, or directly through ferry company websites. Advance booking saves 10-20%.

In Person: Port ticket offices open 1-2 hours before departure. Last-minute availability during shoulder season.

Cost Range: €15-80 per journey depending on distance and speed. Island hopping pass: €100-200 for 5-10 days unlimited travel.

Ferry Travel Tips

  • Arrive at port 30-45 minutes before departure
  • High-speed ferries (hydrofoils) are faster but more expensive and can be rough in swells
  • Regular ferries are slower but cheaper and allow vehicle transport
  • Seasickness is common on rough seas—bring motion sickness medication
  • Ferries run less frequently in winter (November-March)
  • Some ferries have shops and cafes—prices are 2-3x higher than land
  • Baggage allowance is usually generous (1 large suitcase + 1 small bag)

Suggested Island Hopping Itineraries

Classic 7-Day Loop (from Athens): Athens (2 days) → Mykonos (2 days) → Santorini (2 days) → back to Athens (1 day). Ferry cost: approx. $120-180 total.

Comprehensive 10-Day Journey: Athens (2) → Mykonos (1) → Delos (day trip) → Santorini (2) → Paros (1) → Naxos (2) → Crete (2). Ferry cost: approx. $200-280.

Southern Explorer: Athens (1) → Crete (4) → Rhodes (3) → back to Athens. Ferry cost: approx. $150-220.

Cultural Etiquette & Important Tips

Don't miss items on our international travel checklist.

Dress Code & Respect

  • Temples & Churches: Cover shoulders and knees (full-length pants/skirts or sarongs). Removing shoes sometimes required.
  • Beaches: While toplessness is common on some beaches (Mykonos, Crete), it's less accepted in smaller villages. Respect local customs.
  • Restaurants: Casual dining allows minimal dress. Nicer restaurants expect neat casual (no athletic wear).
  • Cultural Sites: Avoid disrespectful behavior at ancient ruins—they're sacred to Greeks.

Communication & Language

  • English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants
  • Learning basic Greek phrases (please=παρακαλώ, thank you=ευχαριστώ, hello=καλημέρα) is appreciated
  • Younger Greeks (under 40) generally speak good English
  • Older locals may speak limited English—patience and smiles help

Greek Dining Etiquette

  • Meals are Social: Greeks take 2+ hours for dinner. Don't rush out.
  • Tipping: 5-10% tip is customary at restaurants. Round up at cafes.
  • Water: Ask for water (νερό) to be served. Tap water is safe to drink.
  • Mezze Culture: Multiple small dishes are ordered to share—embrace it.
  • Timing: Dinner starts late (8-9pm). Lunch is noon-2pm.

General Greek Customs

  • Siesta Time (2-5pm): Many small shops close. Plan accordingly.
  • Hospitality: Greeks are warm and helpful. Accept small gifts (coffee, water) graciously.
  • Photography: Ask before photographing locals or their property.
  • Hand Gestures: The "thumbs up" is okay, but don't show your open palm with fingers spread—it's offensive.
  • Punctuality: Greeks run on "Greek time"—expect delays of 15-30 minutes. Flexibility is key.

Greek Food Guide for Travelers

Also visiting Spain? Read our Spain packing guide.

Must-Try Traditional Dishes

  • Moussaka: Eggplant, meat sauce, and béchamel layered dish. €8-15
  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat skewers with pita, tzatziki. €6-12
  • Spanakopita: Spinach and feta pie in phyllo dough. €5-8
  • Gyros: Seasoned meat wrap with pita. €5-7 (best street food deal)
  • Saganaki: Fried cheese. €8-12
  • Octopus (Htapódi): Grilled or stewed. €15-25
  • Greek Salad (Horiatiki): Tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, feta, olive oil. €8-14
  • Dolmades: Grape leaves stuffed with rice and herbs. €7-10
  • Feta Saganaki: Warm fried feta with honey. €9-13

Beverages

  • Greek Coffee (Elliniko): Strong, thick coffee served in small cups. €2-4
  • Frappe: Iced coffee drink. €3-5
  • Ouzo: Anise-flavored spirit. €3-8 per glass
  • Greek Wine: Excellent local varieties. €5-12 per glass in restaurants
  • Mythos/Alpha Beer: Popular Greek beers. €3-5

Budget Eating Tips

  • Eat where Greeks eat—tavernas near locals, not tourist areas
  • Lunch menus (set meals) offer 30-40% savings: €8-12 for main + sides + bread
  • Souvlaki and gyro stands provide best value: €5-7 for filling meal
  • Supermarket meals (sandwich, salad, fruit): €8-10
  • Avoid menus in English or with pictures outside restaurants—usually overpriced
  • Markets (Laiki Agores) offer fresh produce cheaply: €1-3 per item

Frequently Asked Questions

What do I need to pack for Greece island hopping?
Pack lightweight summer clothing, swimwear, sun protection (SPF 50+), comfortable walking shoes, a reusable water bottle, and quick-dry items for ferry rides. Bring a small daypack for island exploring and consider packing layers for cooler evenings. Compression cubes help maximize luggage space on ferries.
Do US/UK/Australian/Canadian citizens need a visa for Greece?
No visa is required for stays up to 90 days within a 180-day period for citizens of the US, UK, Australia, and Canada. A valid passport (with 6 months validity) is required. Starting 2025, an ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorization System) will be required—it costs €7 (approximately $7.50 USD) and is valid for 3 years.
When is the best time to visit Greece?
April-May and September-October offer ideal weather and fewer crowds. June-August are peak summer season with hot temperatures (85-95°F) and high prices. May and September offer the best balance of weather, amenities, and manageable crowds. For island hopping, May and September are perfect with good weather and full ferry schedules.
How much does island hopping in Greece cost?
Budget travelers can manage $40-60/day. Mid-range travelers should plan for $80-150/day. Luxury travelers should budget $300-700/day. Ferry passes between islands range from $15-50 per journey. A 7-day trip costs approximately $1,000-1,200 USD (excluding international flights) for mid-range travelers including accommodation, food, ferries, and activities.
